# Urge Quiz kiosk — copy strategy

Aligned with Kinnect product voice (warm, brief, specific) while staying **Urge** brand.

## Principles

1. **Warm, brief, one idea at a time** — no wall of text on a booth iPad.
2. **Sentence case** for body and most CTAs. Reserve shouty ALL CAPS for rare moments.
3. **Specific prize language** — “12 monthly boxes · you pick the flavor,” not “amazing prizes!”
4. **Honest stakes** — perfect 10 wins; near-miss still gets the list (no false hope).
5. **Consent is human** — “Yes — email me…” not legal screaming.

## Surfaces

| Screen | Job of the copy |
|--------|------------------|
| Idle | Hook + prize + one CTA |
| Capture | Trust + short form + consent |
| Quiz | Clear question, plain choices |
| Result (win) | Celebrate + what to do next (show staff) |
| Result (near/miss) | Soft landing + still on the list |

## Do not

- Mad-lib product names into every line
- Guilt for missing questions
- Staff jargon on player screens
